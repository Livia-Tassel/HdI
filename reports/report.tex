\begin{abstract}
This study presents a comprehensive analysis of the global health ecosystem using multi-source data spanning 200+ countries over 24 years (2000--2023). We address three dimensions: (1) spatiotemporal disease burden evolution using spatial econometrics (LISA, Moran's I) and panel regression with Driscoll-Kraay standard errors; (2) risk factor attribution via Population Attributable Fractions, Shapley value decomposition, and 20-year policy scenario simulation with Monte Carlo uncertainty; (3) health resource allocation efficiency assessed through Data Envelopment Analysis, four-quadrant classification, and multi-objective optimization. We introduce the Global Health Resilience Index (GHRI), a novel PCA-weighted composite index measuring national health system performance across five pillars. Our causal inference toolkit---including instrumental variables, difference-in-differences, and mediation analysis---goes beyond correlations to identify actionable policy levers. Results provide evidence-based recommendations for intervention prioritization tailored to country income groups and efficiency quadrants.
\end{abstract}

\subsection{Research Questions}

    \item How has the global disease burden evolved spatiotemporally, and what socioeconomic factors drive health outcomes?
    \item What is the quantitative contribution of modifiable risk factors to disease burden, and what would future trajectories look like under different policy scenarios?
    \item How efficiently are health resources allocated globally, and what reallocation would maximize health outcomes while reducing inequality?
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Contributions}
\begin{itemize}

    \item Causal inference beyond correlations (IV, DiD, mediation)
    \item Shapley value decomposition for fair risk factor attribution
    \item Three optimization models for resource reallocation
    \item System dynamics simulation with Monte Carlo uncertainty quantification
\end{itemize}
